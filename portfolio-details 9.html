About this Project :-

This project aims to conduct an in-depth case study of Swiggy's operations and performance by employing SQL queries on a PostgreSQL database, managed through the pgAdmin4 administration tool. The database schema encompasses tables such as Food, Menu, Restaurants, Users, Order-Details, Orders, and Delivery-partners, each playing a crucial role in capturing the intricacies of Swiggy's ecosystem.

Database Design:

The relational database schema is thoughtfully designed to encapsulate the key components of Swiggy's ecosystem:

  1. Food Table: Capturing details about individual food items offered on the platform, including names, types, and prices.
  2. Menu Table: Establishing the relationship between restaurants and the various food items they offer.
  3. Restaurants Table: Encompassing information about the partner restaurants, including names, locations, and contact details.
  4. Users Table: Storing details about Swiggy users, such as usernames, contact information, and preferences.
  5. Order-Details Table: Recording specifics about each item within an order, including quantities, prices, and applicable taxes.
  6. Orders Table: Tracking overarching details about individual orders, such as order numbers, user IDs, and timestamps.
  7. Delivery-Partners Table: Capturing information related to the delivery process, including delivery times, courier details, and delivery statuses.
